Why this platform exists

Commerce careers are broader than one qualification or one job title.

South Africa needs people who can improve business performance, strengthen governance, support SMEs, finance infrastructure, analyse policy, modernise public finance, build ethical companies and use data responsibly. This platform helps users navigate those pathways without forcing them into a single narrow route.

South African relevance

Careers connect to JSE-listed companies, banks, audit firms, mining houses, retailers, telecoms, public sector entities, DFIs, SMEs, startups, NGOs and impact organisations.

Global portability

The same capabilities travel into Africa, Europe, the United States, the Middle East, global multinationals, consulting firms, banks, investment houses and development institutions.

Future pressure

AI, automation, ESG, analytics, regulation and economic change will reward people who combine technical skill with judgment, ethics, communication and adaptability.

Future skills

What commerce careers may look like in 10 years.

AI will not remove the need for commerce professionals. It will change what good looks like. Routine production becomes faster; judgment, ethics, storytelling, stakeholder trust and digital fluency become more valuable.

Automated

Basic reconciliations, first-draft commentary, repetitive reporting, standard tax checks, transaction screening and dashboard refreshes.

Augmented

Forecasting, scenario planning, fraud detection, economic research, policy analysis, valuation support and performance diagnosis.

More human

Ethical judgement, leadership, negotiation, business partnering, ambiguity tolerance, public interest thinking and strategic decision-making.

Learn now

Excel, Power BI, SQL basics, financial modelling, research writing, AI prompting, ESG literacy, communication and portfolio projects.

6-month action roadmap

From interest to evidence.

Month 1

Choose two streams. Read 6 role pages. Save roles that energise you.

Month 2

Build Excel and business writing foundations. Complete one mini analysis project.

Month 3

Learn Power BI or dashboard basics. Create one visual portfolio piece.

Month 4

Complete a role-specific project: tax review, valuation, forecast, credit memo or policy brief.

Month 5

Conduct three career conversations. Refine your CV and interview stories.

Month 6

Apply for internships, graduate programmes, entry roles or internal pivots with evidence.
